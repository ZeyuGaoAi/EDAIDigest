# Cambridge-Initiated AI for Early Cancer Digest

Draft for organiser discussion.

We are proposing a lightweight, outward-facing English digest on `AI for early cancer detection`, initiated from Cambridge, with three sections:

- Papers
- Funding calls
- Job opportunities

The draft would be prepared by an AI agent and reviewed by a human before any distribution.

## Decisions to Make

### 1. Scope

Please confirm what should count as in scope.

Suggested inclusion:

- screening
- early detection
- early diagnosis
- risk stratification relevant to detection
- radiology
- pathology
- liquid biopsy

Suggested exclusion:

- treatment
- metastatic disease
- drug discovery
- general oncology AI without an early-detection angle

### 2. Audience

Who is the main audience for version one?

- researchers
- clinical-academic collaborators
- funders
- industry

### 3. Geography

Which geography should we prioritise?

- global
- UK / Europe
- UK / Europe / US

### 4. Frequency

Suggested default: weekdays only.

### 5. Source List

Suggested starting point:

- Papers: arXiv, PubMed, medRxiv / bioRxiv
- Funding: CRUK, NIHR, UKRI, Horizon Europe, NIH / NCI
- Jobs: university groups, cancer institutes, selected companies, focused job boards

### 6. Review Workflow

Suggested minimum workflow:

1. The system collects new items.
2. The AI agent filters and drafts the issue.
3. A human reviewer approves, edits, or skips.
4. Nothing is sent automatically for now.

We should also confirm:

- primary reviewer
- backup reviewer
- final sign-off

## Proposed Default

Unless others feel strongly otherwise, I suggest:

- Scope: AI for early cancer detection only
- Audience: researchers, clinical-academic collaborators, and funders
- Geography: UK / Europe / US prioritised
- Frequency: weekdays
- Format: `2-4 papers`, `0-2 funding calls`, `0-2 jobs`
- Workflow: AI draft, human review, no automatic sending
- Positioning: a Cambridge-initiated digest with broader external relevance

## Questions for Comment

- Should we include preprints by default?
- Should risk stratification count when clearly linked to early detection?
- Should we include broader AI-health funding calls, or only cancer-specific ones?
- Should jobs include faculty, PhD, and industry roles, or start narrower?
- Are we happy for some issues to contain only papers and no funding/jobs?
- Who will be the primary reviewer and backup reviewer?
